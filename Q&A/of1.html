Q&A for Filipino Caregivers

Q

I have a Caregiving NC II certificate from the Philippines. My Japanese level is basic. How can I work in Japan?

A

If you have a Caregiving NC II certificate, there are several ways to work in Japan as a caregiver.

1. Technical Intern Training (Caregiving)

  • Recommended Japanese level: JLPT N4
  • Working period: up to 3–5 years
  • Usually arranged through a sending organization in the Philippines

After completing the program, many workers change to the Specified Skilled Worker (Caregiving) status.

2. Specified Skilled Worker (SSW – Caregiving)

  • Care Worker Skills Evaluation Test
  • Care Worker Japanese Language Evaluation Test
  • Japanese level around JLPT N4

3. EPA Care Worker Program

This program is operated by the governments of Japan and the Philippines. Participants work while studying Japanese and aim to obtain the Certified Care Worker qualification.

4. Study in Japan

You can study at a Japanese language school or vocational school and obtain the Certified Care Worker qualification.

Tip: Studying Japanese up to JLPT N4 will increase your chances of working in Japan.
